    Historical Note

    The Clinical Research Society was founded in 1920 by a group of medical associates based in New York. Its aim was to facilitate "fellowship and mutual medical enlightenment." The Society held regular meetings featuring scientific programs and discussions on various topics, as well as annual dinners and social outings. In 1925, the Society established a scholarship loan fund for students in the medical colleges of Columbia, Cornell, and New York Universities.

    Collection Description

    Scope and Content

    The collection includes general correspondence and related documents; survey responses from members; minutes and treasurer's reports; correspondence and documents related to loan scholarships; financial documents; constitution and by-laws; programs and meeting announcements; and the early history of the society. The correspondence and miscellaneous documents folder includes published articles by Society members, newspaper clippings, and obituaries.
