Historical Note

The Section on Neurology of the New York Academy of Medicine was established in 1885. In 1897 the section was expanded to include psychiatry. In 1932 some of the psychiatrists petitioned to have a separate section, but the Academy's council decided against the change. In the early 1970s the question of separate sections came up again and this time it was decided to have a section on neurological sciences and a section on psychiatry.

Collection Description

Scope and Content

This collection, which may not be complete at the moment, is particularly interesting. The section's membership included members of the several other groups devoted to neurology and psychiatry whose records are in the possession of the Academy. The membership of the Schilder Society and the Salmon Committee seldom overlapped, but many of the members of both those groups appear in the section records.

Of special interest are the transcripts of the meetings of the section during the 1930s. A regular number of these meetings were combined meetings with the New York Neurological Society. For the most part the minutes in this collection simply record the topics presented and the names of the speakers and discussers. The transcripts record the full content, and were submitted to 2 journals. Another area of interest are the files related to the attempt in the 1930s to have a separate section on psychiatry, an effort that failed. By 1972 the separation was made.

This inventory identifies the records as they reflect the various forms in which this section functioned.
