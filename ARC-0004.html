Historical Note

The Charaka Club was founded in 1898. A group of five doctors: Charles Dana, Joseph Collins, Ward A. Holden, Frederick Peterson, and Bernard Sachs wanted to create a group where they could discuss their interests outside medicine. At first it was called the Medico-Historical Club, but in 1900 the name was changed to The Charaka Club after the legendary Indian physician who compiled a book of the ancient medical texts. The club still exists and meets 4 times a year.

In the early years the notices of meetings were “invitations” to a dinner hosted by a club member at his home. After 1911 meetings were held at the Century Association or other clubs, but after 1923 the regular meeting place was The Coffee House Club. Since 1984 the club usually meets at the Century Association

At the club dinners various members presented papers on literature, art, and history, usually related to medical history. Beginning in 1902 many of these papers were published in The Proceedings of the Charaka Club. Twelve volumes were produced, the last in 1988.

After World War II there was discussion about admitting to membership those who were not MDs. There was concern that the challenges of the post-war medical world might limit the time and leisure doctors had to prepare quality essays. The possibility of dissolving the club was also discussed, but the decision was to continue.

Collection Description

Scope and Content

The activities and interests of the notable physicians who made up the membership of Charaka club are reflected in these records.

The club created various committees, such as Membership and Communications, but there are almost no records of the work of the committees except perhaps through the minutes or executives’ correspondence.

The most informative minutes were kept by Archibald Malloch, 1935-1949. The early minutes are often just notes collected and pasted in a scrapbook and give little information of what transpired at the meetings. This is especially true of minutes from 1962-1989. The secretary gave only a précis of the talk given at the meeting, but little or no information about the business or membership issues that were discussed.

The problem with the minutes is in some ways compensated for by the correspondence of various officers and members. Those of special interest are described in the inventory.

The principle purpose of the club was to share the members’ interests outside of medicine. The talks given by the members at the meetings ranged wildly in subject matter and the club published the best of these talks in The Proceedings beginning in 1902. The last volume was published in 1988 and it will probably be the last as the club is considering circulating the texts electronically.

There are four boxes of texts of many of the talks given after 1925. Some are the texts of talks not included in The Proceedings.

Box 16    Wax impressions of the seal on the signet ring with supporting papers

1 F1925

Wax impression; booklet explaining origin of ring and purpose of wax impression. Given to Charaka archives by George H Walton who had had the ring made. Poem describing seal By Dr. Walton. Copy of letter of Dr. Walton to Dr. Camac.

Charaka Signet Ring was designed and commissioned by Dr. George H. Walton. It was passed from President to President. Some minutes before 1960 report the ceremony of passing the ring to the new president. There is a gap in the minutes for 1959-1960 and the next minutes make no mention of the passing of the ring. The question of what happened to the ring was raised in 1970.
